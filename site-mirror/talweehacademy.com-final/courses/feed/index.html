A Critical Study of Uṣūl al-Shāshī

One of the most essential disciplines within the Islamic sciences—yet often underrepresented in contemporary curricula—is uṣūl al-fiqh (legal theory). Following the successful introduction of a foundational primer in Ḥanafī uṣūl, Talweeh Academy is pleased to offer Level 2, dedicated to a rigorous and in-depth study of the classical text Uṣūl al-Shāshī.

Although widely taught across institutions, Uṣūl al-Shāshī is frequently introduced prematurely or approached through surface-level translation, often without sufficient critical engagement or broader methodological context. This course seeks to address these gaps by offering a structured, analytical, and comparative study of the text—aimed at unlocking its full scholarly potential.

What Makes This Course Unique

This is not a translation-based reading. Rather, it is a critical study and dissection of the text within its proper intellectual and juristic framework. Key features include:

  1. Cross-comparison with other major Ḥanafī uṣūl works
  2. A comparative study with the methodologies of uṣūl al-muta­kallimīn
  3. Critical analysis of the examples and arguments presented in the text
  4. Expansion on essential topics that are often overlooked or insufficiently developed

Who is it for?

This course is intended for students who have successfully completed our Level 1 in Ḥanafī Uṣūl (or an equivalent program) and possess a foundational understanding of the Arabic language.

Those who have not yet completed Level 1 are advised to enroll in and finish the first level before proceeding to this course: 

Hanafi Uṣūl al-Fiqh (Level 01)
 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will:

  1. Develop a strong command of the core discussions in Uṣūl al-Sāshī
  2. Understand the role of the text in shaping the Ḥanafī tradition of legal theory
  3. Recognize key methodological distinctions between the Ḥanafī approach and that of the jumhūr
  4. Analyze and articulate the underlying logical structures of uṣūlī principles

Course Overview:

A comprehensive study of Tajwīd theory, designed to explain the governing principles behind correct Qur’an recitation.

This course provides an extensive study of the science of Tajwīd, the discipline through which the Qurʾān is recited as it was revealed. Rooted in the classical Islamic scholarly tradition, Tajwīd ensures the precise pronunciation and preservation of the Qurʾān across generations.

This program offers structured exposure to all the components of Tajwīd. It is suitable for those seeking to build a solid understanding of the subject, as well as for students who have previously studied Tajwīd and wish to review, organize, or reinforce their knowledge. Covering the core principles of articulation (makhārij), characteristics of letters (ṣifāt), and essential rules of recitation, the course presents Tajwīd in a clear, systematic, and engaging manner.

By the end of the course, students will have gained a well-rounded understanding of the topics of Tajwīd, enabling them to approach Qurʾānic recitation with greater awareness, accuracy, and confidence.

Target Audience

Students Seeking Structured Understanding:
Individuals looking to develop a clear and organized understanding of the principles of Tajwīd.

Review & Refresher Students:

Those who have previously studied Tajwīd and wish to revisit and consolidate their knowledge in a structured format.

Students of All Ages:

The course is designed to be accessible and beneficial for a wide range of learners, accommodating varying levels of familiarity with Qurʾānic recitation.

Course Structure

Foundations of Tajwīd:
The course begins with an introduction to the definition, importance, and legal ruling of Tajwīd, along with the virtues and etiquettes of reciting the Qurʾān. Students will also learn the proper method of beginning recitation through the istiʿādhah and basmalah.

Articulation Points (Makhārij):
A structured study of the articulation points of the Arabic letters, covering the various regions of the mouth and throat. This section provides essential insight into correct pronunciation.

Characteristics of Letters (Ṣifāt):
Students will explore the defining qualities of the Arabic letters, including both opposing and non-opposing characteristics, and how they influence pronunciation and clarity.

Core Rules of Tajwīd:
The course examines key rules such as Ikhfa, Idgham, Qalqalah and the rulings of lām and rāʾ, among others, providing a practical framework for understanding correct recitation.

Rules of Elongation (Madd):
A comprehensive overview of Madd, including its types, causes, and application in recitation.

Waqf and Muṣḥaf Conventions:
The final section introduces the rules of stopping (waqf), their impact on meaning, and the symbols of the muṣḥaf, along with a review of common mistakes and frequently mispronounced words.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will:

  1. Gain a comprehensive overview of the main principles of Tajwīd
  2. Develop familiarity with the articulation points and characteristics of letters
  3. Understand the key rules governing Qurʾānic recitation
  4. Recognize common mistakes and areas of difficulty in recitation
  5. Strengthen and organize their prior knowledge of Tajwīd
  6. Build greater confidence and awareness in their engagement with the Qurʾān

The science of isnād did not cease with the early generations; rather, it evolved into highly structured systems of preservation through athbāt, fahāris, and maʿājim. These works represent a critical yet often underexplored dimension of ḥadīth scholarship, wherein later scholars meticulously documented their transmissions, teachers, and scholarly inheritances.

This course offers a systematic introduction to these later-era sources, focusing on their structure, function, and scholarly significance. Students will learn how to read and interpret these texts, extract chains of transmission, and identify the maqrūʾāt and masmūʿāt within a narrator’s scholarly network. Through guided analysis, the course bridges theoretical understanding with practical application.

A central component of the course is the study of ʿuluww al-isnād, with emphasis on identifying the highest extant chains that retain verifiable samāʿ in the later generations. Students will explore how such chains are preserved, authenticated, and transmitted, engaging with real examples from the scholarly tradition.

Special attention is given to tracing transmission pathways through major later figures, particularly Shāh Walī Allāh al-Dihlawī, whose works serve as a cornerstone in connecting earlier and later isnād traditions, as well as al-Amīr al-Kabīr and other pivotal transmitters. This allows students to situate themselves within a broader historical continuum of knowledge transmission.

The course concludes with a practical demonstration of living chains, including a detailed presentation of transmission routes leading to the Shamāʾil of Imām al-Tirmidhī. This final component provides students with a concrete understanding of how isnād operates not merely as a historical artifact, but as a living and continuous tradition.

Learning Outcomes:

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

Navigate and interpret later-era athbāt and fahāris with confidence
Identify maqrūʾāt and masmūʿāt within complex isnād structures
Trace historically significant high chains with authenticated samāʿ
Recognize and contextualize prominent contemporary musnidūn

Course Overview:

This course provides an in-depth study of al-Suyūṭī’s extensive masterpiece Tadrīb al-Rāwī, one of the most comprehensive works on ḥadīth nomenclature. Building upon the foundational principles acquired in Muṣṭalaḥ 1, students will explore the full spectrum of terminology, classifications, and technical discussions governing the authentication, transmission, and preservation of ḥadīth.
The course emphasizes advanced applications of ḥadīth terminology, examining case studies drawn from classical literature and demonstrating how the rules outlined by earlier scholars are applied in practice. The depth and breadth of Tadrīb al-Rāwī along with Sh. Awwamh’s extensive footnotes will enable students to approach higher-level ḥadīth research with methodological precision.

Learning Outcomes:

Master advanced ḥadīth terminology as presented in Tadrīb al-Rāwī.
Apply the principles of ḥadīth classification to complex scholarly examples.
Analyze and reconcile differences in terminology usage among classical scholars.
Prepare for specialized research in the sciences of ḥadīth.

General Overview

Talweeh Academy is offering a structured and academically rigorous Hadith Specialization Program dedicated to the service of the noble Prophetic tradition. For centuries, scholars and students have devoted their time, resources, and intellect to the preservation, transmission, analysis, and explanation of the Prophet’s (ﷺ) sayings and actions. At Talweeh Academy, we are honored to continue in this noble legacy, striving to be among those who uphold and serve the divinely inspired Sunnah.

This specialization program which ranges over 2 years is designed for students who possess proficiency in the Arabic language, a deep reverence for the science of Hadith, and the ability to engage in independent study and reading. Course Overview:

The science of Jarḥ wa Taʿdīl (الْجَرْحُ وَالتَّعْدِيلُ, al-jarḥ wa al-taʿdīl) stands as one of the most essential disciplines within ʿIlm al-Ḥadīth. It provides the methodological framework through which scholars evaluate the reliability, integrity, and precision of narrators (ruwāt), thereby preserving the authenticity of the Prophetic tradition.

This course offers a structured and in-depth study of the principles governing narrator criticism, guided by the well-regarded work Ḍawābiṭ al-Jarḥ wa al-Taʿdīl by ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz al-ʿAbd al-Laṭīf. Students will engage with both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of the discipline, gaining insight into how classical scholars assessed narrators and resolved complex evaluative differences.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will:

  1. Develop a strong understanding of the foundational principles of narrator evaluation
  2. Distinguish between the various categories and درجات (levels) of jarḥ and taʿdīl
  3. Accurately interpret the technical terminology used by classical ḥadīth critics
  4. Apply principles of fairness and methodological rigor when reconciling differing scholarly assessments
